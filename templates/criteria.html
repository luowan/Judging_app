Judging Criteria

The projects will be evaluated based on the following criteria:

  1. Empower People (40%)

    How well does the project empower individuals and teams within the organization?

  2. Strategic Alignment (15%)

    How closely does the project align with ABAX's strategic goals and objectives?

  3. Innovation & Creativity (15%)

    How innovative and creative is the solution in addressing the problem?

  4. Impact Potential (ROI & Effort) (15%)

    What is the potential return on investment and how feasible is the implementation effort?

  5. Presentation and Demo Quality (15%)

    How well was the project presented and demonstrated to the audience?

ABAX Hackathon Scoring System Rules

The ABAX Hackathon scoring system is designed to fairly and transparently evaluate each project based on judges’ assessments and employee votes. The total possible score for a project is 100 points, divided between judges’ evaluations and employee voting.

1. Judges’ Evaluation (70 Points Total)

  • Number of Judges: 7
  • Scoring Range per Judge: Each judge awards a score between 0 and 10 for each project.
  • Total Judges’ Score per Project: The sum of all judges’ scores, ranging from 0 to 70 points.

Calculation Details

  • Maximum Possible Score from Judges: 7 judges × 10 points = 70 points
  • Minimum Possible Score from Judges: 7 judges × 0 points = 0 points

2. Employee Voting (30 Points Total)

  • Voting Eligibility: All ABAX employees are invited to participate in the voting process.
  • Voting Mechanism: Employees vote for their favorite projects.
  • Points Allocation Based on Employee Votes:
Rank Points Awarded
1st Place 30 points
2nd Place 25 points
3rd Place 20 points
4th Place 15 points
5th Place 10 points
6th Place 5 points
7th Place and below 0 points

Tie Handling: In case of a tie in employee votes, the projects will share the points for the tied positions equally.

Example

If two projects tie for 2nd place, both projects receive:

(25 points for 2nd place + 20 points for 3rd place) ÷ 2 = 22.5 points each.

3. Total Project Score

  • Maximum Total Score: 100 points (70 from judges + 30 from employee voting)
  • Minimum Total Score: 0 points

Final Score Calculation: Total Score for a Project = Sum of Judges’ Scores + Employee Voting Points

4. Determining the Winners

  • Projects are ranked based on their Total Project Scores.
  • The project with the highest total score is declared the winner of the hackathon.
  • Runners-up are determined by descending order of total scores.
  • In Case of a Tie:
    • The project with the higher Judges’ Total Score will be ranked higher.
    • If the tie persists, the projects share the position.

5. Scoring Guidelines

Judges’ Scoring

  • Evaluation Criteria: Judges will score projects based on predefined criteria, such as:
    • Empower People
    • Strategic Alignment
    • Innovation & Creativity
    • Impact Potential (ROI & Effort)
    • Presentation and Demo Quality
  • Scoring Scale:
    • 0: Does not meet the criterion at all.
    • 5: Meets the criterion satisfactorily.
    • 10: Exceeds the criterion exceptionally.
  • Independence: Judges must evaluate projects independently without consulting other judges.
